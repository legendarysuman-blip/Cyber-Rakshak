// Real Incidents

Real-Life Cyber Crime Cases

Documented Indian and global cyber incidents. Each case reveals a different attack method — learn from them to stay protected.

CASE #001 • INDIA • 2022
AIIMS Delhi Ransomware Attack — Nation's Biggest Hospital Held Hostage
RansomwareData Breach
In November 2022, AIIMS Delhi — India's most trusted hospital — was hit by a devastating ransomware attack that crippled all servers for over 15 days. Emergency services, labs, billing, OPD, and patient portals went completely offline. Approximately 3–4 crore patient records were at risk of exposure.

Attackers demanded ₹200 crore in cryptocurrency. The NIA, CBI, IB, and CERT-In were all deployed. Doctors reverted to writing prescriptions by hand. The attack is suspected to have originated from Chinese state-sponsored hackers.
💡 KEY LESSONAlways keep offline backups. Critical systems must be air-gapped from public internet. Never click unknown email attachments — that's how ransomware enters. Install security patches the moment they're released.
CASE #002 • INDIA • ONGOING
Jamtara Phishing Syndicate — India's Cyber Crime Capital
PhishingBanking Fraud
Jamtara, a small district in Jharkhand, became India's notorious "phishing capital." Organized gangs of teenagers with ₹500 SIM cards would call victims posing as bank officials, KYC agents, or even police officers — tricking them into sharing OTPs and debit card details.

Thousands of victims across India lost lakhs of rupees each. Over 800 arrests were made by 2022. Netflix's documentary series "Jamtara: Sabka Number Ayega" brought national attention. Operations have since spread to Mewat, Mathura, and other regions.
💡 KEY LESSONNo real bank or government official will EVER ask for your OTP, CVV, or PIN over the phone. The moment someone asks — hang up immediately. Call your bank's official number printed on the back of your card.
CASE #003 • INDIA • 2023
CoWIN Data Breach — Vaccination Portal Exposed Millions
Data BreachPrivacy Violation
In June 2023, a Telegram bot was found providing personal details of Indian citizens registered on the CoWIN vaccination portal — including Aadhaar numbers, phone numbers, and passport details of senior government officials and ministers.

Data of potentially millions was exposed. CERT-In and the Ministry of Health launched investigations. The government stated CoWIN databases weren't directly breached but data was likely stolen from healthcare workers' devices. It raised urgent questions about India's data protection framework.
💡 KEY LESSONNever share government portal credentials with anyone. Use strong unique passwords and enable 2FA on all government portals. India's DPDP Act 2023 now mandates strict data protection — companies face ₹250 crore penalties for breaches.
CASE #004 • GLOBAL • 2017
WannaCry Ransomware — The Attack That Hit India's Banks & Police
RansomwareGlobal Impact
WannaCry infected 230,000 computers across 150 countries in May 2017 — demanding Bitcoin ransom. In India, Andhra Pradesh Police computers, electricity boards, and major banks were affected. The UK's NHS cancelled 19,000+ appointments. Global damages reached $4–8 billion.

It exploited a Windows vulnerability called EternalBlue — which Microsoft had patched 2 months earlier. North Korea's Lazarus Group was blamed. A 22-year-old British researcher accidentally stopped the attack by registering a domain hidden in the malware's code.
💡 KEY LESSONAlways install Windows and software updates IMMEDIATELY — WannaCry only succeeded because people ignored a patch released 2 months earlier. Enable automatic updates on all devices.
CASE #005 • INDIA • 2021–2022
Bulli Bai & Sulli Deals — Targeted Harassment of Muslim Women
CyberbullyingPrivacy Violation
Two GitHub-hosted apps — "Sulli Deals" (2021) and "Bulli Bai" (2022) — listed photos of hundreds of Muslim women without consent, presenting them as "auction items." The apps were built by engineering students using public social media photos.

Multiple arrests were made across states under IT Act Sections 66E and 67. GitHub removed the repositories. Cases led to nationwide debate about India's need for comprehensive online harassment laws under the new DPDP Act 2023.
💡 KEY LESSONNon-consensual use of photos is a criminal offence under IT Act Section 66E and IPC 354C. Set all social media profiles to private. Report such content immediately at cybercrime.gov.in with screenshots as evidence.
CASE #006 • INDIA • 2022–2024
Pig Butchering Investment Scams — Indians Lose ₹4.45 Crore
Investment FraudSocial Engineering
Thousands of Indians lost crores to "pig butchering" scams — where fraudsters build romantic or friendly relationships over weeks, then convince victims to invest in fake cryptocurrency trading platforms. Victims see convincing fake profits before losing everything.

One Mumbai case saw a 68-year-old retired person lose ₹4.45 crore. Scam operations run from Southeast Asia using trafficked workers. CBI launched Operation Chakra targeting these transnational networks in 2023.
💡 KEY LESSONAny investment promising guaranteed high returns — especially from a new online "friend" — is a scam. Verify all platforms with SEBI before investing. Never invest based on advice from strangers you met online.
// Legal Framework

Indian Cyber Laws

Know your rights and the laws that protect you. India has comprehensive cyber legislation — understanding it makes you a stronger digital citizen.

Your Digital Rights Are Protected By Law

India's Information Technology Act 2000 (amended 2008), Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023, DPDP Act 2023, and POCSO Act 2012 collectively form a powerful legal shield for every Indian citizen online.

💻 IT ACT 2000
Information Technology Act, 2000 (Amended 2008) — India's Primary Cyber Law
▼

India's primary legislation governing electronic commerce, digital signatures, data protection, and cyber crimes. The 2008 amendment vastly expanded its scope to address modern threats.

SEC 43
Unauthorized Access & Damage
Penalizes unauthorized access, downloading data without permission, introducing viruses, or disrupting computer networks.
⚡ Civil compensation up to ₹1 crore
SEC 66
Computer Related Offences (Hacking)
Dishonest or fraudulent acts of hacking, data theft, system damage, or unauthorized access to computer systems.
⚡ Up to 3 years + ₹5 lakh fine
SEC 66C
Identity Theft
Fraudulently using another person's electronic signature, password, OTP, Aadhaar, PAN, or unique ID feature.
⚡ Up to 3 years + ₹1 lakh fine
SEC 66D
Cheating by Personation Online
Creating fake profiles, impersonating someone online, or committing fraud by pretending to be another person.
⚡ Up to 3 years + ₹1 lakh fine
SEC 66E
Violation of Privacy
Capturing, publishing, or transmitting private intimate images or videos of a person without their consent.
⚡ Up to 3 years + ₹2 lakh fine
SEC 66F
Cyber Terrorism
Attacks threatening national security, sovereignty, or critical infrastructure — power grids, banking, defence systems.
⚡ LIFE IMPRISONMENT
SEC 67
Publishing Obscene Material
Publishing or transmitting obscene material in electronic form — websites, social media, messaging platforms.
⚡ Up to 5 years + ₹10 lakh fine
SEC 67A
Sexually Explicit Material
Publishing sexually explicit content electronically. Covers revenge porn and non-consensual intimate image sharing.
⚡ Up to 7 years + ₹10 lakh fine
SEC 67B
Child Sexual Abuse Material
Creating, browsing, downloading, or distributing CSAM in any electronic form. Zero tolerance under Indian law.
⚡ Up to 7 years + ₹10 lakh fine
SEC 72
Breach of Confidentiality
Any official who discloses electronic records, accounts, or data accessed in discharge of duties without consent.
⚡ Up to 2 years + ₹1 lakh fine
📜 BNS 2023
Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023 — Cyber-Related IPC Provisions
▼

The new Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (replacing IPC 1860) contains several sections directly applicable to online crimes, often used alongside the IT Act for comprehensive prosecution.

BNS 318 (IPC 420)
Online Fraud & Cheating
Covers UPI fraud, fake e-commerce, online financial scams, and any cyber-enabled cheating or dishonest inducement.
⚡ Up to 7 years + fine
BNS 356 (IPC 499/500)
Online Defamation
Defamatory social media posts, fake news, false statements damaging someone's reputation online.
⚡ Up to 2 years + fine
BNS 351 (IPC 503/506)
Cyberstalking & Online Threats
Threatening messages, cyberstalking, intimidating emails, or creating fear through social media platforms.
⚡ Up to 7 years
BNS 77 (IPC 354C)
Voyeurism
Recording or disseminating intimate images of women without consent. Covers hidden cameras and sextortion material.
⚡ 1–7 years depending on offence
🔒 DPDP 2023
Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 — Your Data Rights
▼

India's landmark data privacy law enacted August 2023. Gives every Indian citizen significant rights over their personal data and imposes heavy obligations on companies.

YOUR RIGHT
Right to Know
You have the right to know what personal data any company holds about you and why it's being processed.
⚡ Companies must respond to your requests
YOUR RIGHT
Right to Correction & Erasure
You can demand companies correct inaccurate data or delete your data when no longer needed for its purpose.
⚡ Companies must comply
PENALTY
Data Breach Penalties
Companies that fail to protect your data or don't notify you of a breach face massive financial penalties.
⚡ Up to ₹250 crore per breach
CHILD SAFETY
Children's Data Protection
Processing data of children under 18 requires verified parental consent. Behavioural tracking of children is prohibited.
⚡ Up to ₹200 crore for violations
👶 POCSO 2012
POCSO Act 2012 — Online Child Safety Provisions
▼

POCSO extends fully to the online world. Online grooming, digital sexual abuse, and CSAM are treated with the same severity as physical offences.

SEC 13
Use of Child for Pornography
Using a child in any digital media for pornographic purposes — including apps, social media, or encrypted platforms.
⚡ Minimum 5 years, up to life imprisonment
SEC 15
Storing Child Abuse Material
Possessing CSAM on any device — phone, computer, or cloud storage — is criminal even without distribution.
⚡ 3–5 years imprisonment + fine

Think Before You Click

The #1 rule. Before clicking any link:

1Hover over link to see real destination URL
2Check for misspellings — paypa1.com ≠ paypal.com
3Look for HTTPS and padlock icon
4When in doubt — type the URL manually
🔄

Keep Everything Updated

Outdated software is the #1 entry point for attackers.

✅Operating System (Windows/Android/iOS)
✅Web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari)
✅Antivirus and security software
✅Banking and payment apps
✅Social media apps
💾

Regular Data Backups

Ransomware is powerless against good backups.

1Follow 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 formats, 1 offsite
2Use Google Drive or iCloud for auto backup
3Keep monthly external hard drive backup
4Test your backups — can you actually restore?
📡

Safe WiFi Practices

Public WiFi is a hacker's hunting ground.

✅Use mobile data for banking tasks
✅Use a VPN on any public network
✅Disable auto-connect to open networks
❌Never access banking on public WiFi
🔐

Strong Password Formula

✓Minimum 12 characters — 16+ is better
✓Mix UPPER, lower, numbers and symbols (!@#$)
✓No dictionary words or personal info (name, birthday)
✓Unique password for EVERY account — never reuse
✓Use a passphrase: "Tiger@Runs#Fast!2026"
🗝️

Use a Password Manager

Remember just ONE master password — let the manager handle the rest.

1Install Bitwarden — free, open-source, trusted globally
2Generate unique 20-character passwords for every site
3Let it autofill — never type passwords manually
4Store master password somewhere safe offline
📲

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA blocks 99.9% of automated attacks.

🥇Use Google Authenticator or Authy app
🥈SMS OTP — better than nothing
✅Enable on Gmail, WhatsApp, Instagram, banking
❌NEVER share 2FA code with anyone
🕵️

Check for Leaked Passwords

1Visit haveibeenpwned.com — enter your email
2If found in breach — change that password immediately
3Change ALL accounts that used the same password
4Check regularly — new breaches happen constantly
📱

Secure Your Smartphone

✅Use strong PIN or biometric lock — not 0000
✅Enable full-disk encryption (Settings → Security)
✅Only install apps from Play Store / App Store
✅Enable Find My Device and remote wipe
❌Never root or jailbreak your phone
🔍

App Permission Audit

1Go to Settings → Apps → Permissions
2Ask: Does a flashlight app need contacts? NO
3Revoke location access from non-navigation apps
4Delete apps you haven't used in 3+ months
💻

Secure Your Computer

✅Enable Windows Defender or reputable antivirus
✅Enable firewall — always on
✅Use standard user account, not Administrator
✅Enable BitLocker disk encryption
✅Screen lock after 5 minutes idle
🌐

Browser Security

1Use Chrome or Firefox — always updated
2Install uBlock Origin — blocks malware ads free
3Enable Safe Browsing in Chrome settings
4Use password manager — don't save in browser
🔒

Privacy Settings Audit

1Set all profiles to Private immediately
2Remove phone number from public profile
3Disable location tagging on posts
4Review Connected Apps — remove unknown ones
🤐

What NOT to Share Online

❌Aadhaar / PAN card photos — ever
❌Bank account screenshots or details
❌"Currently on vacation" posts in real time
❌Home address or school/workplace location
❌Children's photos with location/school tags
🚫

Spot Fake Profiles

🚩Profile created recently with few posts
🚩Asking for money, gift cards, or crypto
🚩Romantic interest moving unusually fast
🚩Always has excuses not to video call
✅Reverse image search their profile photo
🏦

UPI Safety Rules

✅You NEVER need PIN to RECEIVE money
✅Always verify UPI ID before sending
✅Set daily transaction limits in UPI app
❌Never share UPI PIN on any phone call
❌Decline all unknown collect requests
❌Never install AnyDesk at anyone's request
💳

Card & Net Banking Safety

1Enable virtual card numbers for online shopping
2Set lower daily limits on debit/credit cards
3Block international transactions if not needed
4Never use net banking on shared computers
5Check statements weekly for unknown transactions
🆘

If You're Scammed — Act Fast!

1Call 1930 IMMEDIATELY — every minute matters
2Block your card via app or customer care
3Screenshot all evidence: chats, IDs, numbers
4File complaint at cybercrime.gov.in
5Visit nearest cyber police station
